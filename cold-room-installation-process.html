What to Expect During a Cold Room Installation

Whether you run a butchery in Lusaka, a lodge in Livingstone, a supermarket on the Copperbelt or a farm needing storage for produce, a cold room is a major investment that has to be done right. Knowing how the process unfolds helps you plan, budget and avoid surprises. Here's exactly what to expect, stage by stage, when Siams RB builds your cold room.

The stages of a cold room project

  • 1. Site assessment and sizing
  • 2. Design and quotation
  • 3. Selecting panels and refrigeration plant
  • 4. Site preparation
  • 5. Panel assembly and door fitting
  • 6. Refrigeration installation and commissioning
  • 7. Testing, pull-down and handover
  • 8. Ongoing maintenance
  • Typical timeline

1. Site Assessment and Sizing

Every successful cold room starts with a visit to your site. We measure the available space, look at access for delivery and assembly, check the power supply and assess the ground or floor. Just as importantly, we talk through what you'll store, in what quantity, and how often you'll be loading and unloading.

This is where sizing happens. A butchery storing carcasses, a supermarket holding dairy and a farm chilling vegetables all need different volumes, temperatures and door sizes. Getting this right from the start avoids the costly mistake of a room that's too small within a year, or an oversized one that wastes power. If you'd like to understand the numbers before we visit, our cold room sizing guide explains how volume and load are calculated.

2. Design and Quotation

With the measurements and requirements in hand, we design the room: its dimensions, the operating temperature (chiller versus freezer), panel thickness, door type and position, and the refrigeration capacity needed for the Zambian climate. We then provide a clear, itemised quotation so you know exactly what you're paying for — panels, refrigeration plant, electrical work, installation and commissioning.

This is the stage to ask questions and finalise details: shelving, ramps, strip curtains, lighting and any future expansion you might want to allow for.

3. Selecting Panels and Refrigeration Plant

Cold rooms are built from insulated sandwich panels — typically PUR or PIR foam between steel skins. The thickness is chosen to suit the temperature: thicker panels for freezer rooms, thinner for chillers. In Zambia's heat, good insulation directly cuts your running costs, so this is not the place to cut corners.

The refrigeration plant — the condensing unit and evaporator — is sized to hold temperature even on a 35-degree October afternoon. We also factor in load-shedding resilience here, advising on backup power so your stock survives ZESCO outages. The door, hinges and seals are selected for heavy daily use.

Worth knowing: Specifying slightly more refrigeration capacity and better insulation at the design stage costs a little more upfront but protects your stock during long, hot load-shedding spells — when a marginal system would struggle.

4. Site Preparation

Before panels go up, the site must be ready. This covers a level, suitable floor (with insulated flooring for freezer rooms to prevent the ground freezing), proper drainage for defrost water, and an adequate electrical supply run to the location. For larger installations we coordinate any civil work needed. Good preparation here prevents problems later, such as a door that won't seal on an uneven floor or a unit tripping an undersized circuit.

5. Panel Assembly and Door Fitting

Now the room takes shape. The insulated panels are locked together — floor (where fitted), walls and ceiling — using cam-lock joints that pull the panels tight for an airtight, seamless seal. Our team levels and squares everything as it goes, because a cold room that isn't true will leak cold air and waste energy.

The door is fitted last and carefully aligned so its heated frame and seal close perfectly every time. A well-hung door is one of the most important parts of an efficient cold room.

6. Refrigeration Installation and Commissioning

With the shell complete, the refrigeration system goes in. The condensing unit is mounted (often outside or in a ventilated plant area), the evaporator is fitted inside the room, and the pipework and electrical connections are run between them. The system is then evacuated, charged with refrigerant and wired to its controls, including the thermostat and defrost timer. Drainage from the evaporator is connected so defrost water clears away cleanly.

7. Testing, Temperature Pull-Down and Handover

Before we hand over, the room is thoroughly tested. We run the system through a full pull-down — bringing the empty room down from ambient to its target temperature — and confirm it holds steadily. We check the door seal, the defrost cycle, the alarms and the controls, and verify the electrical work is safe and correct.

At handover we walk you through operating the room: how to set and read the temperature, how to load it without overloading the system, what the alarms mean, and basic daily care. You receive the documentation for your equipment, and your team knows how to get the best from the room.

8. Ongoing Maintenance

A cold room is a long-term asset, and like any refrigeration system it performs best with regular care. Dust clogs condenser coils, seals wear, and small faults left unchecked become expensive failures — and a failed cold room can mean a freezer full of spoiled stock overnight. We recommend putting your room on a scheduled service plan. Our maintenance contracts cover planned visits and priority emergency support, so your room keeps running through the seasons.

Typical Timeline Expectations

Timelines vary with size and complexity, but as a rough guide: the assessment and quotation usually take a few days to a week. Once the order is confirmed and materials are on hand, a standard small-to-medium cold room is often assembled and commissioned within a few days to about two weeks. Larger industrial rooms, custom builds, or projects requiring civil works and special panel orders take longer. Site preparation, lead times on imported refrigeration plant, and access at your premises all affect the schedule, which is why we confirm realistic dates at the quotation stage.

Built Right, From the Start

A cold room installation is a partnership: the better we understand your needs and site, the better the room we build. From the first assessment to the final handover and beyond, Siams RB manages every stage so you end up with a cold room that holds temperature, survives load-shedding and serves your business for years. Whether you're in Lusaka, on the Copperbelt or out in Solwezi, our cold room installation service covers the whole country.
